Paneer Butter Masala

Paneer Butter Masala is a popular North Indian dish known for its rich, creamy, and mildly spiced tomato-based gravy. It is made using soft paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cooked in butter, cream, and aromatic spices, giving it a smooth and flavorful taste. This dish is widely loved for its slightly sweet and buttery texture and is commonly served with naan, roti, or rice in Indian meals.

  1. Heat butter in a pan.
  2. Add onion and sauté until golden.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and tomato puree. Cook well.
  4. Add spices and salt.
  5. Add paneer cubes and cook for 5 minutes.
  6. Add cream and mix well. Serve hot.

Vegetable Pulao

Vegetable Pulao is a simple and flavorful Indian rice dish prepared by cooking basmati rice with a variety of fresh vegetables, aromatic spices, and herbs. It is known for its light taste and pleasant aroma, making it a healthy and quick meal option. Vegetable pulao is often served with raita, salad, or pickle and is commonly enjoyed in Indian households as a balanced and nutritious dish.

  1. Wash and soak rice for 20 minutes.
  2. Heat oil, add cumin seeds and onion.
  3. Add vegetables and sauté.
  4. Add rice, water, and salt.
  5. Cook until rice is soft. Serve warm.

Aloo Paratha

Aloo Paratha is a popular North Indian flatbread stuffed with a spiced mashed potato filling. It is made using whole wheat dough filled with seasoned potatoes, then rolled and cooked on a hot pan with butter or ghee. Aloo paratha is loved for its crispy outside and soft, flavorful inside. It is commonly served with curd, pickle, or butter and is a traditional breakfast dish in many Indian homes.

  1. Prepare dough using wheat flour and water.
  2. Mix potatoes with spices.
  3. Stuff potato mixture inside dough ball.
  4. Roll into paratha shape.
  5. Cook on tawa using butter/ghee. Serve hot

Masoor Dal (Red Lentil Curry)

Masoor Dal (Red Lentil Curry) is a simple and nutritious Indian dish made from red lentils cooked with onions, tomatoes, and mild spices. It has a soft texture and comforting flavor, making it a staple in many Indian households. Rich in protein and easy to digest, masoor dal is commonly served with steamed rice or roti as a healthy everyday meal.

  1. Wash and pressure cook dal with water and turmeric.
  2. Heat oil, add cumin seeds and onion.
  3. Add tomato and cook well.
  4. Add cooked dal and salt.
  5. Simmer for 5 minutes and serve.

Suji Halwa

Suji Halwa is a traditional Indian sweet dish made from semolina (suji), ghee, sugar, and water. It has a soft, smooth texture and a rich, aromatic flavor, often enhanced with dry fruits like cashews and raisins. Suji halwa is commonly prepared during festivals, religious occasions, and special family gatherings, and it is loved for its warm, comforting sweetness.

  1. Heat ghee and roast suji until aromatic.
  2. Boil water and add to suji carefully.
  3. Add sugar and mix continuously.
  4. Cook until thick.
  5. Add dry fruits and serve warm.
